DIABETES PREVENTION

Get smart about risks and diabetes prevention. With early detection and awareness, you can take steps to prevent or delay the onset of type 2 diabetes.

Small Changes Lead To Big Results.

Small changes to your lifestyle can help to prevent or delay diabetes, even if you’ve been diagnosed with prediabetes. Your doctor will help you create a plan and set goals that work for you. They may also refer you to a Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) recognized, evidence-based lifestyle change program. Don’t hesitate to ask for help along your journey.

Learn About Lifestyle Change Programs

Here, you will find comprehensive information about Lifestyle Change Programs designed to help individuals reduce their risk of developing type 2 diabetes through healthy lifestyle modifications. Our Lifestyle Change Programs are rooted in evidence-based practices and aim to empower participants to make sustainable changes in their dietary habits, physical activity levels, and overall behavior. By promoting healthy living and fostering supportive environments, these programs offer individuals the tools and guidance they need to take proactive steps towards preventing diabetes and improving their overall well-being. Explore the resources available on this page to learn more about program eligibility, components, success stories, and how to enroll. Together, we can work towards a healthier future free from the burden of diabetes.
Overweight? Know the impact.

Welcome to our "Overweight" section, where we explore the critical link between weight management and diabetes prevention. Being overweight or obese significantly increases the risk of developing type 2 diabetes and other chronic health conditions. Understanding the impact of excess weight on your health is the first step toward making positive lifestyle changes. In this section, we delve into the effects of excess weight on diabetes risk, the role of lifestyle modifications in prevention, and practical strategies for achieving and maintaining a healthy weight. Let's embark on a journey toward improved health and well-being by exploring the connection between weight management and diabetes prevention.
Need reasons to quit Smoking?

Quitting smoking is a pivotal step towards improving your overall health and reducing the risk of various chronic diseases, including diabetes. Smoking not only damages your lungs and respiratory system but also significantly contributes to the development of insulin resistance, a key factor in the onset of type 2 diabetes. The harmful chemicals in tobacco smoke impair the body's ability to utilize insulin effectively, leading to elevated blood sugar levels and an increased risk of diabetes complications. Moreover, smoking exacerbates existing health issues commonly associated with diabetes, such as cardiovascular diseases, kidney problems, and nerve damage. It accelerates the progression of diabetic complications, making it harder to manage the condition effectively.

High blood pressure

High blood pressure, also known as hypertension, is a prevalent health concern affecting millions worldwide. It is often referred to as a 'silent killer' because it typically presents with no noticeable symptoms, yet it significantly increases the risk of serious health complications, including heart disease, stroke, and kidney damage. In the context of diabetes management, understanding and addressing high blood pressure is paramount, as individuals with diabetes are particularly vulnerable to its adverse effects. At DROP, we recognize the critical importance of managing high blood pressure as part of a comprehensive approach to diabetes care. Through education, awareness, and proactive measures, we aim to empower individuals to take control of their blood pressure levels and reduce their risk of diabetes-related complications. Let's explore the link between high blood pressure and diabetes and discover effective strategies for prevention and management
